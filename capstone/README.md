# Capstone: 90-Day Adoption Roadmap

> **Level:** Expert
> **Prerequisite:** All 10 modules complete. `checklists/advanced-completion.md` fully checked.
> **Verified:** 2026-04

This is an open-ended capstone — there is no solution directory. Your roadmap is specific to your context. What it must contain is defined here. What it says is yours.

---

## Objective

Produce a written, committed, self-assessed 90-day Copilot adoption plan that applies every substantive skill from this course to a real project context.

When done, you will have a single document that:

- Shows where you are now across all 4 course levels
- Commits to specific, verifiable milestones at 7, 30, 60, and 90 days
- Chooses 3 KPIs you will actually track
- Defines the governance policy appropriate to your team size
- Has been self-assessed against `checklists/expert-completion.md`

## The 3 Required Artifacts

### Artifact 1: `capstone/roadmap.md`

A single markdown file. It must contain all 5 sections:

**Section 1 — Current state self-placement**

For each of the 4 course levels, write 1–3 sentences of evidence:

| Level | Skills covered | My evidence |
|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Beginner | Modes, configuration, cost awareness | [your evidence] |
| Intermediate | Prompt engineering, custom instructions | [your evidence] |
| Advanced | Agents, multi-agent workflows | [your evidence] |
| Expert | Advanced features, repository quality, governance | [your evidence] |

Evidence must be specific. "I feel comfortable" does not count. "My prompt library has 7 entries committed to `prompts/`" counts.

**Section 2 — 90-day roadmap**

Four rows, one per time horizon. Each row must have a gate deliverable that is a specific file or checklist — not an intention.

| Timeframe | Focus | Gate deliverable |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| 7 days | [your focus — aligned to your weakest Level 1–2 skill] | [specific file or checklist item] |
| 30 days | [your focus — aligned to your Level 2–3 gaps] | [specific file or checklist item] |
| 60 days | [your focus — aligned to your Level 3–4 gaps] | [specific file or checklist item] |
| 90 days | [your focus — full integration and team rollout readiness] | [capstone complete + expert-completion.md] |

**Section 3 — KPI selection**

Name your 3 KPIs. For each rejected metric, write one sentence. Format: match Exercise 2 in `modules/10-adoption-roadmap/exercises.md`.

**Section 4 — Governance policy**

Select your policy tier. Write a minimum 5-rule policy. Commit it here if you are working solo; commit it to your project's `CONVENTIONS.md` if working in a team context.

**Section 5 — Review cycle commitment**

State when your monthly review will fall (e.g., "First Monday of each month"). Name who is responsible for each of the 4 steps. If solo, you are responsible for all 4.

## Success Criteria

The capstone is complete when:

1. `capstone/roadmap.md` exists with all 5 sections filled in — no `[your evidence]` stubs remaining

---

## Common Failure Points

| Failure | Signal | Recovery |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Writing for an idealized self | Day 7 tasks require Level 3 skills you don't have yet | Rewrite Day 7 for your actual current level; move aspirational tasks to Day 30 |
| Vague gate deliverables | "Improve my prompting" with no file or checklist | Replace with a specific artifact — "5 prompts committed to `prompts/`" |
| Skipping KPI rejection | Only naming 3 KPIs without explaining what was rejected | Exercise 2 answers are your model; all 7 rejections must be explicit |
| Leaving expert-completion stubs blank | Difficult items left unchecked with no note | Every gap requires a one-line remediation action — even "revisit Module 06 exercises" is sufficient |
| Writing the roadmap last | Capstone treated as a summary of what was done | The roadmap is forward-looking from today; sections 1 and 2 are distinct |
